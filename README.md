# Synthesized-Clinical-Notes-Multimodal-AI-Models
Paper journal npj Digital Medicine (under revision).

## Abstract
Various types of biomedical metadata, such as images, signals, and structured and unstructured clinical notes collectively provide comprehensive information on the patient. Deep learning models that combine these modalities in their training tend to result in improved predictive and analytical performance. However, many biomedical datasets are unimodal and relatively small which inhibits multimodal model development. Public skin lesion datasets, for instance, include only images grouped under a categorical disease label and have relatively small number of samples per disease condition. The paper presents an approach for generating multimodal data by pairing unimodal datasets with synthesized clinical notes (textual descriptions) generated by state-of-the-art Large Language Models (LLMs). The proposed multimodal architecture trained using this synthesized data is evaluated on five tasks: image classification, cross-modal retrieval, zero-shot learning, visual question answering, and text generation. Multiple strategies are tested for generating notes, considering potential LLM limitations such as domain shift and hallucinations. Results show that small unimodal datasets can be transformed into effective multimodal resources to train robust and accurate algorithms across multiple medical AI applications.
